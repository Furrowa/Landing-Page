India’s agriculture sector is showing strong momentum in 2026. Google Trends data reveals consistently high interest in "Agriculture in India" throughout April and May, as farmers search for practical solutions to tackle water scarcity, unpredictable weather, and rising costs.

Small and marginal farmers are at the forefront of adopting simple yet powerful agritech tools that deliver real results in the field.

Current Challenges in Indian Agriculture

Farmers across the country are facing:

  • Irregular monsoons and depleting groundwater
  • High electricity and diesel costs for irrigation
  • Risk of overwatering or underwatering crops
  • Need for better yields from limited land

These challenges explain the rising curiosity around modern yet affordable farming technologies.

The Power of Smart Irrigation

Smart irrigation uses real-time soil moisture data to give precise watering advice. This approach helps farmers:

  • Save significant amounts of water
  • Improve crop health and yield
  • Reduce irrigation-related expenses
  • Make confident, data-backed decisions
