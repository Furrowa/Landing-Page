Drip Irrigation Cost Per Acre in India (2026)

Costs vary by crop, terrain, pipe quality, and emitter type. As a general guide:

  • Basic system (vegetables, flat terrain): ₹20,000 – ₹30,000 per acre
  • Mid-range system (cotton, sugarcane): ₹30,000 – ₹45,000 per acre
  • Advanced system (orchards, complex terrain): ₹45,000 – ₹60,000+ per acre

These are one-time capital costs. Annual maintenance is typically 5–10% of the initial investment.

Government Subsidies Available

Under PMKSY (Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana) and state-level schemes, subsidies of 45–55% are available for small and marginal farmers. In some states, eligible farmers receive up to 65% subsidy. The net out-of-pocket cost can fall to ₹10,000–₹25,000 per acre — making drip irrigation accessible for most farmers.

The Hidden Waste: Running Drip at the Wrong Time

After installing a drip system, the biggest ongoing cost is pump electricity. Most farmers run their drip system on a fixed schedule — every 2–3 days regardless of actual soil conditions. This means:

  • Running the system when the soil is still adequately moist (wasted electricity)
  • Overwatering, which promotes root disease and nutrient leaching
  • Missing the optimal irrigation window, reducing yield
