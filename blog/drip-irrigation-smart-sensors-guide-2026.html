What is Drip Irrigation and How Oriva Makes It Smarter (2026 Guide)

May 1, 20266 min read

Drip irrigation is one of the most water-efficient methods available to Indian farmers today. By delivering water directly to the root zone through pipes and emitters, it reduces evaporation and runoff significantly — saving 30–50% water compared to flood irrigation.

But even the best drip system has one unsolved problem: how do you know exactly when to turn it on, and for how long? Most farmers use fixed schedules or gut feel — and that leads to overwatering, wasted electricity, and reduced yield.

How Drip Irrigation Works

  • Water flows from a source through a main pipeline
  • Sub-main pipes branch out across the field
  • Lateral lines run along crop rows with emitters at each plant
  • Water is delivered drop-by-drop directly at the root zone

The result is consistent root-zone moisture, reduced weed growth, lower disease risk, and higher fertiliser efficiency when nutrients are added through the system.

The Missing Piece: Knowing When and How Long to Irrigate

Even with a perfect drip setup, farmers face a daily question: Should I irrigate today? And for how many minutes?

Without actual soil data, the answer comes from habit or calendar — neither of which accounts for recent rainfall, current temperature, or actual crop demand. This is the gap Oriva fills.
