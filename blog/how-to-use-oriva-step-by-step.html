How to Use Oriva Portable Irrigation Advisory System – Step by Step

May 1, 20264 min read

Getting started with Oriva is simple. No installation, no wiring, no internet connection required. This guide walks you through exactly how to use Oriva Portable Irrigation Advisory System and get your first accurate watering recommendation.

What You Need

  • Your Oriva device
  • A smartphone with the Oriva App installed (available for Android and iOS)
  • Bluetooth enabled on your phone — that's it

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Carry Oriva to Your Field

Oriva is compact and portable. Carry it to the spot in your field where you want to check soil moisture — typically in the root zone of the crop you are monitoring. You can take readings at multiple spots across the field.

Step 2: Insert the Probe in the Root Zone

Push the Oriva probe into the soil at root depth — the zone where your crop absorbs water. No digging is needed. The probe is designed for easy insertion even in compact or hard soil.

Step 3: Open the Oriva App and Connect via Bluetooth

Open the Oriva App on your smartphone. Enable Bluetooth — the app connects to the device automatically. No internet connection is needed. No SIM card. The connection is instant and local.

Step 4: Get Your Recommendation

The app instantly shows you two key pieces of information:

✅ When to water — should you irrigate today, or wait?
✅ How long to water — exact duration (e.g., "Irrigate for 40 minutes")

No numbers to interpret. No guesswork. Just a clear, actionable instruction in your preferred language.

Tips for Best Results

  • Measure at multiple spots — fields are not uniform; a corner patch may be drier than the centre
  • Measure at root depth — insert the probe fully so the sensors reach the active root zone
  • Measure before each irrigation decision — soil conditions change after rain, heavy sun, or previous irrigation
  • Compare zones — if your field has different soil types or slopes, measure each zone separately

Works Offline. Supports Multiple Languages.

Oriva operates entirely via Bluetooth — no internet connectivity is needed at any point. The Oriva App is available in multiple Indian languages so recommendations are always clear and accessible, regardless of the farmer's literacy in English.
